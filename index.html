Research project · 2025–2027

Enabling Grid and Customer Benefits
via Virtual Energy Sharing

A research collaboration between KTH, E.ON Energy Networks, and the Swedish Energy Agency, developing business models, behavioral analysis, and a digital platform for virtual energy sharing in Swedish distribution networks.

KTH Royal Institute of Technology
E.ON Energy Networks
Funded by Swedish Energy Agency

About the Project

Despite regulatory advances in the European Union under the Renewable Energy Directive (RED II) and the Internal Electricity Market Directive, Sweden lacks concrete operational guidance for virtual energy sharing.

This project develops a forward-looking business model framework tailored to Swedish regulation, grid constraints, and customer rights. The work spans regulatory mapping, stated-preference behavioral analysis, data-driven flexibility modeling, grid impact simulation on real low-voltage feeders, and a multi-layer integrated assessment — validated in a pilot site within E.ON's distribution network in southern Sweden.

Project Milestones

Six deliverables across the 18-month timeline.

M1 Feb 2026

Business Models & Scenarios

Two to three business models with governance and settlement mechanisms, plus at least four energy sharing scenarios tailored to Swedish contexts.

M2 May 2026

Behavior & Flexibility Models

Survey-based behavioral analysis and data-driven flexibility models for BtM and FtM customer-side resources.

M3 Oct 2026

Digital Platform & Grid Impact

Digital platform quantifying grid performance — peak reduction, voltage stability, hosting capacity — across sharing configurations.

M4 Mar 2027

Integrated Assessment

Selection of the business model and scenario that maximize system and customer benefits, supported by multi-layer technical-economic-regulatory-social evaluation.

M5 Mar 2027

Pilot Calibration

Calibration of the digital platform in a real-world pilot within E.ON's distribution network, preferably in Skåne.

M6 Mar 2027

Implementation Guide

Final implementation guide with validated results, policy recommendations, and replication potential.

Customer Survey Platform

A stated-preference instrument supporting WP2.

The platform implements a stated-preference survey for assessing customer willingness to participate in virtual energy sharing initiatives. Participants respond under hypothetical scenarios spanning political, economic, societal, technical, and environmental factors.

Results inform the behavioral analysis in WP2 and the socio-economic benefit assessment in WP4, complementing data-driven flexibility models built from smart meter and DER metadata.

Currently in pilot calibration with E.ON. Public participation opens after pilot validation.

Network Analysis Platform

A distribution-network simulator supporting WP3.

A distribution-network simulator aimed at DSOs and system planners, for analysing how virtual energy community sharing affects the low-voltage grid. Users build a feeder topology, configure load groups and behind-the-meter assets, and run a real power flow to reveal how community sharing reshapes transformer loading, voltages, losses, and grid exchange across a 24-hour cycle — comparing the network with and without VEC.

This supports the grid-impact analysis in WP3, complementing the customer-side behavioral study.

Pilot Site

Real-world validation in southern Sweden.

[Pilot photo — to be provided by E.ON]

E.ON owns large parts of the grid in southern Sweden, where energy capacity and production challenges are most critical. A representative pilot site within this network — preferably in Skåne — will host the real-world validation.

The pilot features mixed residential and commercial/industrial customers with complementary energy profiles, coordinating solar PV, batteries, EVs, heat pumps, and district heating connectivity based on smart meter data and DER operation logs. All data handling follows GDPR and unbundling rules.
